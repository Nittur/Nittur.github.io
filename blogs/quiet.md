# Quiet

Between Christmas and New Year there's a week that doesn't feel like real time.

Offices are empty. Notifications slow down. The year is technically still running but everyone has silently agreed to pause it.

I've started protecting this week deliberately.

## What I Did

Nothing, mostly. Slept past 9. Read books that had nothing to do with anything useful. Went on a walk without headphones, which felt radical.

I played Elden Ring for four hours on a Tuesday afternoon without guilt.

## What I Noticed

The thoughts that arrive in quiet are different from the ones that arrive during noise. They're slower. They connect things.

During the year, I'm processing inputs. During quiet, I'm synthesizing them.

> You can't hear yourself think if you never stop making noise.

## Carrying It Forward

I don't think "I should be quieter" is an insight worth anything unless it changes behavior.

So: one hour a day with no inputs. No podcast, no video, no music. Just walking or doing dishes or whatever.

It's harder than it sounds. Silence feels like something needs filling.

That discomfort is probably the point.
